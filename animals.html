The Cattle in Our Care

Every animal here has a story. These are a few of the cows and bulls sheltered at the panjrapole.

Healthy

Gauri

Gir · Indigenous
4 years 420 kg

Story: Found abandoned near a roadside and brought to the panjrapole, weak and malnourished.

Care: Daily green fodder, mineral supplements and regular health checks.

Recovering

Nandi

Kankrej · Bull
6 years 580 kg

Story: Injured on a busy road and brought in for treatment by well-wishers.

Care: Wound dressing, rest and a strengthening diet while he recovers.

Healthy

Lakshmi

Indigenous · Cow
3 years 350 kg

Story: Surrendered by a family who could no longer keep her during a dry season.

Care: Calcium-rich feed and grazing twice a day in the gaushala fields.

Healthy

Krishna

Indigenous · Calf
8 months 120 kg

Story: An orphaned calf found alone in a field and raised in the panjrapole's calf shed.

Care: Bottle feeding, warmth and the company of other calves.

Recovering

Parvati

Indigenous · Cow
12 years 390 kg

Story: An old cow left behind once she stopped giving milk, now cared for in her later years.

Care: Soft fodder, shade and gentle handling suited to her age.

Critical Care

Shiva

Kankrej · Bull
7 years 510 kg

Story: Rescued with deep wounds and infection after a fall, now under close watch.

Care: Intensive veterinary treatment, fluids and round-the-clock monitoring.
