About the Panjrapole

A community gaushala at Mandal, dedicated to the shelter and seva of cattle for generations.

Our Purpose

Mission & Vision

Mission

To shelter, feed and care for abandoned, old, sick and injured cattle for the whole of their lives, upholding the tradition of jivdaya and gauseva with dignity and compassion.

Vision

A community where no cow is abandoned or left to suffer — where caring for cattle is a shared responsibility and a living tradition passed to the next generation.

What Guides Us

Our Values

Jivdaya

Compassion for all living beings — every animal is treated with gentleness and respect.

Seva

Selfless service to the cattle in our care, carried out every day without exception.

Community

Devotees, donors and volunteers who keep the panjrapole running together.

Self-reliance

Traditional gaushala practices that put cow dung and gomutra to good, sustainable use.

Our Journey

A Tradition of Care

1972

Founded as a charitable trust

Established by the local mahajan community at Mandal to give shelter to cattle that could no longer be cared for elsewhere.

1999

Recognised by the Animal Welfare Board of India

The panjrapole was registered as a recognised Animal Welfare Organisation by the AWBI, Government of India.

Growing

Expanded shelter & feeding

Sheds, water points and fodder storage were expanded over the years to care for a growing herd.

Today

Hundreds of cattle in care

The panjrapole continues its daily seva — feeding, treating and sheltering cattle with community support.
