Our Story

Shri Mandal Mahajan (Panjrapole)

Shri Mandal Mahajan (Panjrapole) is a charitable trust at Mandal village, on the Ahmedabad–Viramgam–Shankheshwar Tirth highway. For nearly 197 years it has given lifelong shelter to abandoned, old, sick and helpless animals — caring for every jeev until its last breath.

The panjrapole shelters around 1,500 animals at its Mandal site and up to 6,000 animals at its Ugharoj Veed branch. During 2024–25 it cared for roughly 6,000 cattle on average.

Mandal is a place of deep heritage — the birthplace of the ministers Vastupal & Tejpal, who built the famous Delvada (Dilwara) temples, and home to Shri Gadliya Parshwanath Dada among the 108 Parshwanaths. The sanstha runs by the inspiration, guidance and blessings of Ba. Bra. Shri Gokulbhai Shah, a disciple of Shrimad Rajchandra.

Our Purpose

Mission & Vision

Mission

To shelter, feed and care for abandoned, old, sick and injured cattle for the whole of their lives, upholding the tradition of jivdaya and gauseva with dignity and compassion.

Vision

A community where no cow is abandoned or left to suffer — where caring for cattle is a shared responsibility and a living tradition passed to the next generation.

Our Journey

A Tradition of Care

c. 1829

Founded by the Mandal mahajan

Established nearly 197 years ago by the mahajan community at Mandal to give lifelong shelter to cattle that could no longer be cared for elsewhere.

Heritage

Land of Vastupal & Tejpal

Mandal is the birthplace of the ministers who built the Delvada temples, and home to Shri Gadliya Parshwanath Dada among the 108 Parshwanaths.

1 Apr 2001

Dignity in death

The trustees unanimously ended the contractor-sale of deceased animals. Since then, every animal that passes away is given a proper, dignified burial.

10 Dec 2022

Honoured by the Governor

Gujarat Governor Shri Acharya Devvrat visited the panjrapole at a farmers' felicitation gathering and contributed ₹5,00,000 to the seva.

2024–25

~6,000 animals cared for

Around 6,000 animals were maintained on average across the year, each looked after with feeding, water and treatment until its last breath.

Facilities

Features of the Panjrapole

How the gaushala shelters, feeds and treats thousands of animals across its Mandal site and the Ugharoj Veed branch.

Shelter for 10,000

Capacity to house up to 10,000 animals across the Mandal site and the Ugharoj Veed branch.

Sheds & Water

412 permanent (pucca) sheds and feeding bays, served by three borewells for a steady water supply.

24-hour Vet Care

A veterinary doctor on call round the clock and a daily dispensary for the herd.

Care for the Sick

Separate sheltering, soft bedding and a dedicated water arrangement for sick and weak animals.

Fodder Godowns

Large permanent godowns store green and dry fodder so the cattle are fed twice a day, all year.

Drought Relief

In times of drought, the panjrapole has sheltered 8,000 to 10,000 animals brought in from the region.

Rescue

Countless stray, abandoned and injured animals are rescued by the caretakers and given a safe home.

Dignified Burial

Animals that pass away are never sold — each is given a proper, respectful burial on suitable ground.
